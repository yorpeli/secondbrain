## Key Achievements

**Rollout** — We increased CLM traffic in all Tier 1 and 2 Countries to 25% mid-October. Setting us for a global increase for 25% as part of the overall CLM rollout plan.

**POR document approval performance** — We have seen significant improvement across several top tier countries. Auto approval rates increased from 51% → 72%, resulting in a 60% reduction in document re-open rates, enhancing both customer experience and reducing operational load. Auto-approval rates tripled in Argentina and Vietnam and doubled in Colombia and Mexico.

**Account setup completion rate is up** — We have seen an 8% increase globally this month compared to September – 48% > 56%. Representing the rate of users passing through the account setup portion of the flow and signing our T&C, leading them to documents upload portion of the onboarding flow. We expect this increase to translate to an overall increase in approval rates.

**CLM Partners Registration** — Achieved good results on CVR, when looking at the approval rate for the 7 Partners rolled out. When comparing performance with the GLPS Opt-in for the same partners, the approval rates remain consistent — 28% for CLM versus 29% for the 4-step flow. This is the first milestone of expanding CLM to non-D2P onboarding (outside Canada license) as part of our commitment to support all onboarding flows that require KYC.

**October Approval Rates** — In the last 2 weeks of October we have seen roughly 5%-10% increase in approval rates. While very preliminary and still being analyzed we believe this is a result of the improvement in Bot screening. We will continue to track and analyze and will update in the next newsletter.

## Year-End Priorities and Next Steps

As we near the end of 2025, CLM focus continues to be gearing up for a full rollout. Our efforts in the next months will be completing capabilities migration from 4Step to CLM and optimize approval rates and revenue on CLM flows. Some of the efforts coming up are:

**Routing users from mobile to desktop** — CVR for CLM users using mobile devices is very low. The trend is clear especially for tier 1-2 companies with 3% approval rate on avg. on mobile app and 8% on mobile web in the last 2.5 months (vs. 32% on desktop). The direct unequivocal causes are unclear and therefore there are no quick solutions to the problem, we will, therefore, encourage users getting to mobile as an entry point to continue their onboarding flows on desktop.

**Transition of KYC management from InRule to P&Q** — Currently, both InRule and P&Q enforce KYC logic independently, leading to fragmented decision paths. The upcoming transition will strengthen compliance governance, simplify audits, reduces duplication of customer requirements, and improves operational accuracy.

**Increase onboarding CVR by 3% by replacing 'Showroom'** — Replace with access to a lean version of Payoneer account (without the ability to use the services). We intend to complete this by EOY. Why is this important? We believe that providing customers with the initial value of accessing the platform and exploring our offering will increase their motivation to complete onboarding.
