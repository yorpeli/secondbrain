## Key Achievements

### T1 Localization

Successfully launched the Brazil eKYB collection POC. While still with small registration numbers, two weeks live showing encouraging early results. 23 customers have signed up and were prefilled with company information sourced from local government database with a 100% successful response rate. Customers who approved the prefilled data required two fewer documents for submission and time to submission was very short (we will update on time to approval and additional metrics in the next newsletter).

We have also launched the UK eKYB collection POC last week.

### Vendor Optimization

Rolled out Colombia/Mexico eKYC/ePOR with Persona (~30% ePOR match rate and up to 6% increase in account approval in these countries). We are now rolling out to Thailand, Israel, and United Arab Emirates.

Starting a comparison test between Trulioo and Persona in Brazil, Turkey, Australia. Results show match rate improvement – Brazil 70% in Persona vs 30% in Trulioo, Turkey 41% in Persona vs 1% in Trulioo.

**POR improvements:** Address accuracy increased from 76% to 95% through insights and country specific configurations. Expected to launch this month, this improvement is expected to reduce reopen rate by 7% and increase POR approval by 10% - this is expected to translate to an uplift of 1% to CLM conversion rate.

The Human-In-The-Loop (HITL) flow supporting UiPath was stopped in early December, reducing document review time from up to 3 days to less than 1 day. Approximately 17K monthly tickets transitioned from partial HITL reviews - which previously took several days - to full manual KYC reviews completed in under 24 hours.

### New KYC CX

**FDC file submit page UI experiment** was deemed successful as it delivered 1% increase in overall approval and 2% in company type account approval.

**CVD localization experiment** was deemed unsuccessful since the test group underperformed relative to the control group across all primary metrics. We are now conducting a deep-dive analysis to identify specific friction points and "the why" behind the data. This will allow us to refine our approach and return with a more effective product solution.

## Looking Ahead

### Rollout & Enterprise Partners

Partner rollout phase 2 is scheduled for January 19. It will include all non-Lite Plan Tier 3 partners, 30% of non-Lite Plan Tier 2 partners, and 10% of non-Lite Plan Tier 1 partners, along with the Banking partnership and PWP programs, with some exceptions (for example, JazzCash). The rollout will be gradual, allowing impact to be measured before scaling up the volume for Tier 1 and Tier 2 partners.

Rollouts for eBay, Etsy, and BestBuy are planned for early February, following the migration of Profiles and Qualifications, and the deployment of AHA features on January 18, since these updates are a prerequisite for the rollout.

### T1 Localization

Ongoing discovery across the United States, Singapore, and India.

**United States:** Collaborating with Compliance on quick win initiative reducing EIN document requirement, with potential for ~2% company CVR uplift and up to a 7-day reduction in approval time.

**Singapore:** Progressing with evaluation of local eKYB vendors assessing data quality and coverage.

### Vendor Optimization

We continue expanding our reach for additional vendors as part of our multi vendor strategy. We plan several new vendor POCs to ensure we have the best match rates and performance for the rollout. (Sumsub/Shufti/IDMerit/AiPraise/Persona for selfie verification).

Ongoing vendors verification monitoring will be launched in January to analyze vendors key results including true positive and true negative rates.

### China and Hong Kong

Initial rollout in China is planned before EOM. We're currently in internal testing and expect to perform more rigorous tests in the upcoming weeks.
