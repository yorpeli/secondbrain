# Meeting with Nir Mayyan (Forter)

## Company Context

- Forter is ~500 people total, ~200 in engineering, ~15-16 PMs
- Significantly smaller than Payoneer — calibrate insights accordingly

## Key Learnings

- **PMs write code** — small fixes, PRs for low-impact changes. Rationale: the team moves at the speed of its slowest part, so remove bottlenecks by letting PMs self-serve on small things.
- **Engineers do ideation** — but risk is they sometimes fall in love with ideas, which needs active management.
- **"Productize the process"** — the standout quote. Don't build a linear, gated process that acts like a cop ("you can't do that"). Instead, build a product that guides people through what's needed, adapting to the situation and team.
- **Replaced all external tools** — Asana, Google Docs, Excel all replaced with a single internal platform built specifically for their needs. Runs internally (not a SaaS dependency).
- **From NPI to adaptive flow** — they had a linear "New Product Introduction" process (like Payoneer's DLC). Replaced it with an adaptive system that adjusts to context.
- **Idea lifecycle in the platform**: company-wide idea submission → PM + engineer validate against company goals → maturation via voting → tracking through delivery.
- **Gamification was huge for adoption** — once they gamified the process and made it engaging, adoption took off.
- **Automation removed friction** — biggest win was that the process tells you what to do next, so you don't have to figure it out each time.

## Planning Framework: "From Company Goals to Roadmap"

A ~6-month planning cadence (Jul–Jan) across five parallel swimlanes with cross-lane feedback loops. Not a one-shot waterfall — multiple validation and revalidation checkpoints.

**Swimlanes:**

| Swimlane | Flow |
|----------|------|
| **Roadmap Progress** | Company Goals + R&D Requirements → Product Goals + Flagships + KRs → All Initiatives + KRs → Bottlenecks + Prioritization → Detailed Roadmap |
| **Product** | Internal Product Brainstorming → R&D Offsite → Customer Validation + Revalidate Product Goals → Detailed Roadmap Breakdown |
| **Product - GTM** | Dialogue with GTM Leadership (Key Themes) → GTM Leadership dialogue (Oct) → GTM Leadership dialogue (Dec) |
| **Product - Management** | QBR → Management provides high-level Company Goals → QBR → Present Roadmap to MGMT → MGMT QBR |
| **Management** | Initial Work on Company Goals → Extended Mgmt offsite → Mgmt Offsite |

**What they added (marked "NEW" in their diagram):**
- Customer validation + revalidation of product goals (Sep–Oct)
- Multiple GTM leadership dialogues as checkpoints (not just one handoff)
- Management providing high-level company goals earlier in the cycle

**Key design principle:** Cross-lane dependencies via feedback loops (blue arrows in diagram) — e.g., management goals feed product brainstorming, customer validation feeds back into roadmap progress. The process is iterative, not sequential.

## Relevance to AI-Native Team Model

- The "productize the process" concept aligns with building AI-native workflows — the tooling shapes behavior, not policies
- PM-as-coder is a data point for the blurred role boundaries thesis
- Internal tooling investment signals that off-the-shelf tools weren't sufficient for their operating model
