# Meeting with Yuval Nissan (Wix)

## Key Learnings

- **AI adoption is a transition, not a switch** — engineers vary in readiness; takes time.
- **The paradigm is a moving target** — build flexible, don't over-invest in any specific tool.
- **Repos must be AI-ready** — `claude.md`, `agents.md`, architecture docs, skills protocol. Must be tool-agnostic (not locked to Cursor). Requires cross-repo access, internal MCPs, and tooling.
- **Kill the FE/BE distinction** — move toward a single org model. Train engineers for proficiency at everything; the agent fills the gaps.
- **Builder/Creator role split**:
  - **Builder** (from Engineer) — builds infrastructure, repos, agents
  - **Creator** (from PM) — uses AI tools to create independently, without sending code
- **PM "maturity" = Creator** — a PM who operates without depending on engineering for execution. No barrier for PMs — remove friction, give them access and tools.
- **Two PM camps emerging at Wix**:
  - **UX camp** — focused on product/user experience
  - **Data camp** — focused on analytics, metrics, data warehouse
- **Analysts + Product working together** closely in this model.

## Notes

## Relevance to AI-Native Team Framework

- Strongly validates the **Builder/Creator split** in our thinking.
- Repo readiness emphasis (claude.md, skills protocol, tool-agnostic) aligns with infrastructure-first approach.
- The "no barrier for PM" thesis maps directly to the Creator role definition we're developing.
