# Hiring Process for AI Talent — Product Guild

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Participants:** Yonatan Orpeli (VP Product, CLM), Mor Regev Lalush (Head of Product Guild), Noa Lichtig (Talent Acquisition)
**Context:** Designing the hiring process specifically for AI-native talent roles (Principal PM + Director) currently in active recruitment via iTalent. Follows from the operating model article's call to adapt hiring for pod talent.

## Background

Two active hiring tracks for the AI-native team:
1. **Principal PM (IC)** — senior individual contributor
2. **Director** — team leader (Yonatan skeptical this profile exists yet — people who *used* AI tools ≠ people who *led* with AI-first mindset)

Current Payoneer hiring process is too long, not all existing interviewers are suited for evaluating AI talent, and there's a risk of the "halo effect" (candidates who say the right AI buzzwords getting fast-tracked without real validation).

## Key Decisions

### Process Structure: 2 Days (Not 1)

Agreed on a **minimum 2-day process** with a gap day between:

- **Day 1:** Sense interview + Execution interview
- **Gap day:** Internal debrief, due diligence, logistics for Day 2
- **Day 2:** Leadership soft skills + VP panel (2-3 VPs in a challenging conversation)
- If Day 2 passes → **offer can go out that same day**

Rationale for not closing in 1 day:
- Mor: Candidates deserve a second chance if Day 1 was an off day; multiple sessions give more signal
- Noa: Payoneer has a pattern of "halo effect" — falling for candidates who say the right words. Need structural safeguards against this.
- Yonatan: Even if Day 1 is amazing, don't close same day. But don't lose them to bureaucracy either.

Scheduling: Both days should be within the same week. Tell candidates upfront: "If you pass Day 1, Day 2 is on [date]."

### Screening

- Done upfront, before the 2-day on-site
- For referral candidates or people already known → screening can potentially be skipped
- Mor's team handles screening

### Interview Stages — What Changes

| Stage | Status | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **Sense** (product sense) | Keep as-is | Cleanest signal, no modifications needed. "In the AI era, sense only gets amplified." |
| **Execution** | Rethink for AI context | Current format tests standard PM execution. Needs to evaluate AI-specific capability — these candidates are being hired for a specific AI mandate. "Above and beyond table stakes." Not just "can you ship" but "can you ship *with AI as a first-class tool*." |
| **Leadership** | Sharpen soft skills focus | Tailored to new team dynamics (3-person pods, shared ownership). Connects to work with Hadas on the Wiz-or-Working track. Soft skills that matter: chemistry in small teams, handling ambiguity, co-creation mindset. |

### Interviewer Panel

- Need a **curated, specific list** of interviewers for these roles
- Not everyone in the current interviewer pool is suited to evaluate AI talent
- VP panel on Day 2: 2-3 VPs who can genuinely challenge the candidate (not a rubber stamp)
- These interviews are **top priority** — override other interviews, courses, delivery for the interviewers involved

### AI Tool Budget for Candidates

- Give candidates **~$100 gift card** for AI tool tokens (Claude, Cursor, etc.)
- Rationale: If a candidate built their workflow on Claude Code, don't force them to switch tools for the execution exercise. Let them use their own setup.
- Mor to explore logistics (gift cards, vouchers — Payoneer may have relevant product here)
- This also signals to candidates that Payoneer takes AI seriously

### Priority & Framing

- These AI hires are the **#1 talent acquisition priority**
- Mor: "This pushes everything — other interviews, AI courses, delivery. This is the top priority. But only for these specific roles, not all hiring."
- Interviewers must understand this is "red carpet" / top-priority treatment
- Not about lowering the bar — about removing friction while keeping rigor

## Halo Effect Warning (Noa)

Payoneer has an organizational tendency to create the "halo effect":
- Candidate says the right AI words → everyone falls in love → hire fast → towers built on assumptions → towers collapse
- Structural safeguard: never close in one day, have senior/experienced interviewers, challenge candidates in the VP panel
- The 2-day structure is partly a defense against this
