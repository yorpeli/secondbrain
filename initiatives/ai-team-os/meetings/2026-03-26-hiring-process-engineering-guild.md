## Process Structure (Tomer)

Engineering hiring for "Wizards" (AI talent) follows an accelerated, multi-stage process:

1. **Online screening ("Fair Pass")** — initial technical filter, done remotely
2. **On-site technical interview** — with dedicated laptops provided for hands-on work
3. **HR interview** — cultural fit and soft skills assessment
4. **Team fit** — matching to specific team/pod
5. **Immediate offer** — if all stages pass, offer goes out right away

Key design principle: **minimize time from "we want this person" to "here's the offer."**

## Differences from Product Hiring Process

Tomer noted the main differences between product and engineering hiring for AI talent:
- **Interviewer pool size** — engineering has a larger pool but needs careful curation for AI roles
- **Cadence** — engineering can potentially run interviews on consecutive days
- For a small number of "Wizards," the accelerated process works similarly to the product track

Both tracks converge on the same principles: speed, curated interviewers, top priority, immediate offers.

## Key Decisions

### Pre-Built Compensation Package
- Build a **known comp package for Wizards in advance** so offers can go out immediately without additional approval cycles
- **Ilona** (Platform org, manages all Platform roles and comp approvals) to prepare this
- This removes one of the biggest bureaucratic bottlenecks

### Dedicated AI Assessment
- Tomer to develop a **dedicated technical assessment for AI Wizards**
- Separate from the standard engineering assessment — needs to evaluate AI-specific capability
- Parallels the product side's decision to rethink the execution interview for AI context

### Interviewer Prioritization
- All relevant interviewers **tagged and allocated as high-priority** for Wizard interviews
- Entire system mobilizes: logistics (workstations, equipment), scheduling, availability
- Same principle as product side: these hires override everything else

### HR + Team Fit: Keep Separate
- Discussed whether HR interview and team-fit interview could be combined
- **Agreed to keep them separate** — different evaluation criteria, different interviewers

### Start with One, Then Expand
- Tomer recommended **hiring one AI talent first** and evaluating impact before scaling
- Rationale: avoid a situation where multiple hires get stuck due to organizational barriers
- Agreed: proceed carefully but decisively — don't let caution become paralysis

## Cultural Change

Yonatan emphasized that bringing real AI talent into Payoneer will require cultural change:
- These people bring a fundamentally different approach to work
- They can be growth engines for the org — but only if given room to run
- Organization must **allow them to move fast and break existing barriers**, not constrain them into current processes
- This connects to the operating model's thesis: AI-native teams need different governance, not more governance
