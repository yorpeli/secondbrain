09 / 24
The work itself

HR work is shifting. This is where we add value now.

Recruiting

Sourcing & screening compress. Interviews stay human. Calibration gets harder — every candidate has a co-pilot.

→ Spend the freed time on signal, not throughput.
HRBP / PP

Faster diagnostics — sentiment, churn risk, benchmarks in minutes. The conversation with the manager is the strategic value.

→ Show up with a point of view, not a deck.
Org excellence & strategy

Execution is automated. Value shifts to insight.

→ The differentiator isn't access to data — it's what you do with it.
Workplace

Logistics are increasingly automated. What remains is judgment and designing the employee experience.

→ What matters, when it matters, and why — that's still human.

Examples, not the full map — L&D, total rewards, talent and more are shifting too.

10 / 24
The headcount question

It's not "fewer people." It's "different people."

01

Different skill mix.

The roles that compress are the ones AI does well. The roles that grow direct AI, design workflows, and own judgment. Composition changes — not headcount.

02

Shift, not delete.

AI is 5.7× more likely to shift responsibilities than to eliminate a role.

SHRM, 2026

03

New roles are appearing.

30+ new job titles in HR and IT alone in the last 18 months — AI enablement leads, workflow designers, automation owners.

The reframe

Fewer of some seats. More of others. Same total headcount — very different team.

What we need from people

The bar moved for everyone — but it moved differently.

Individual Contributors

From executing tasks → directing AI to execute
From deep specialty only → T-shaped: depth + adjacent fluency
From "what should I do?" → "here's what I decided"
From waiting for clarity → operating with ambiguity

Leaders

From assigning work → designing the workflow AI and humans share
From managing throughput → managing taste and standards
From reviewing artifacts → evaluating judgment calls
From team-as-pyramid → team-as-pod
What grows for both — soft skills
Communication Judgment Curiosity Comfort with ambiguity Standards & taste
12 / 24
Team shapes

Smaller teams. Wider scope. More T-shaped people.

01

The pyramid is flattening.

Fewer entry-level "do the task" roles. More "design the workflow" roles.

02

Specialists are bundling.

A "PM" today is PM + research analyst + light designer + light data analyst. A recruiter is recruiter + market analyst + content creator + employer-brand strategist.

03

Pods over departments.

Cross-functional 3–8 person units out-shipping 30-person orgs. Seed-stage AI startups ship to millions with 3–5 engineers.

The skills that just appreciated.

01

02

03

AI doesn't hear hesitation in a 1:1. It doesn't read the room. Still the job.

04

Taste & standards.

When everyone can produce something passable, the people who know what good looks like become the constraint.

Internal — what's changing in Platform

Different structure. Different lifecycle. Different muscle.

01

A new "Core" domain.

A dedicated domain for the shared substrate every product team builds on — data, AI, foundational services. Stop reinventing, start composing.

→ New role archetypes need clear ladders and rubrics.
02

A different dev lifecycle.

Faster iteration. Prototype-first. AI in every step from design → spec → code → review → deploy.

→ "What did they decide?" replaces "How polished was the artifact?"
03

Education & enablement.

Foundry turns AI capability into shipped impact across functions. The community spreads it horizontally.

→ Hiring profiles, onboarding, growth conversations should all point here.

Your playbook

Five things to start this quarter.

01

Re-read every JD.

Half describe a job that's already changed shape. Start with the highest-volume reqs.

02 · Recruiters

Partner earlier with hiring managers.

Push them on the right skills and soft skills, not just titles. Most calibration problems start in debriefs that haven't caught up.

03

Update calibration language.

"What did they decide?" beats "How polished was the artifact?"

04

Build AI-fluency signal into interviews.

Not a tools test — a judgment test. "Tell me about a time you overrode an AI suggestion. Why?"

05

Partner earlier on org design.

Don't be the team handed the new shape. Be the team that helps draw it.
