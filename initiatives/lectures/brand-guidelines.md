# Payoneer Brand Guidelines

## Brand Foundation
Mission: Connect the world's underserved businesses to a rising global economy
Vision: A more reliable and connected cross-border economy
Brand Promise: We remove the friction between ambition and achievement so businesses can create and capture opportunities across borders
Brand Position: For businesses anywhere, the Payoneer financial stack makes it easy to operate in a global economy that feels borderless—so they can capture and create opportunity more freely
Brand Pillars

Efficiency: We make it easier to grow and operate a global business
Reliability: We make the cross-border economy more reliable
Flexibility: We make businesses more connected

Visual Language Principles
1. Minimalistic

Clarity builds trust
Strip out anything non-essential
Embrace white space
Keep designs sharp, focused, and purpose-driven

2. Product-led

Reflect digital platform nature
Use UI design structure and hierarchy
Show product in context with real users

3. Authentic

Use real, relatable imagery
Highlight people, places, and workspaces
Reflect reality of diverse global customers

4. Monochrome

Black and white foundation (70-90% of color volume)
Secondary colors used sparingly (10-30%)
Accent colors to highlight and drive actions

Logo
Components:

Halo: Full-color circular symbol representing universal availability
Wordmark: Solid black or white

Versions:

Horizontal logo (standard for most uses)
Vertical logo (square/vertical formats)
Halo symbol alone (avatars, small spaces)

Partnership Usage:

Payoneer leads when partnership is equal or favored
Visual separation line between logos required
Maintain clear space

Color Palette
Primary Colors (70-90% usage)

Charcoal Black (#1E1E28) - Reliability & Strength
Pure White (#FFFFFF) - Clarity & Simplicity

Secondary Colors (10-30% usage)

Midnight Blue (#002373) - Trust & Expertise
Electric Blue (#0033FF) - Efficiency & Innovation
Neon Purple (#977DFF) - Flexibility & Connectivity
Hazy Pink (#FFCCF2) - Human-Centric & Approachable

Extended Palette (for themed applications)

Off-White (#F8F9FC) - Card backgrounds, content areas
Light Gray (#EEEEF2) - Borders, dividers, subtle separation
Mid Gray (#888899) - Secondary text, labels, captions
Text Dark (#2D2D3A) - Body text on light backgrounds
Halo Orange (#FF6B35) - Gradient accent, energy moments
Halo Green (#00C875) - Gradient accent, success states

Theme System
The Payoneer visual identity supports two complementary themes. Both use the same core color palette but apply it differently. Choose the theme based on context and communication goal.

Dark Theme (Original)

When to use: Hero moments, presentations, title slides, keynotes, premium/flagship communications, event branding
Base: Charcoal Black (#1E1E28)
Text: Pure White (#FFFFFF) for headings, Mid Gray (#888899) for secondary
Primary accent: Neon Purple (#977DFF) — used for highlights, badges, accent text
Secondary accent: Electric Blue (#0033FF) — used for interactive elements, links
Decorative elements: Circle outlines and dotted patterns in low-opacity purple/blue (rgba overlays at 20-25%)
Cards/containers: Not typically used — content sits directly on dark background
Gradient (CTA banners): Midnight Blue (#002373) → Charcoal Black (#1E1E28)
Logo: White wordmark on dark background

Light Theme

When to use: Documents, internal communications, trackers, one-pagers, infographics, reference materials, web-style layouts
Base: Pure White (#FFFFFF)
Content areas: Off-White (#F8F9FC) with Light Gray (#EEEEF2) borders — rounded corners (12-14px radius, referencing Halo)
Text: Charcoal Black (#1E1E28) for headings, Text Dark (#2D2D3A) for body, Mid Gray (#888899) for labels and secondary
Primary accent: Multi-color gradient — Electric Blue (#0033FF) → Neon Purple (#977DFF) → Halo Orange (#FF6B35) — used for hero text, title treatments, featured badges
Category color-coding: Midnight Blue for L1/primary tier, Electric Blue for L2/secondary tier, Neon Purple for L3/tertiary tier, Charcoal for advanced/builder tier
Cards: Off-White (#F8F9FC) background, 1px Light Gray border, 12px rounded corners
Pill labels: Tinted background matching category color at ~15% opacity, category color text, 4px rounded corners
Featured sections: Charcoal (#1E1E28) background with white text — use for key outcomes, CTAs, or summary blocks
Gradient (CTA banners): Electric Blue (#0033FF) → Neon Purple (#977DFF) — bright gradient, white text, used for forward-looking/teaser sections
Logo: Full-color Halo + black wordmark on white background

Theme Mixing Rules

A single asset can use both themes — dark for header/footer "sandwich" with light for content body
Never use the gradient title treatment (multi-color) on dark backgrounds — it's a light theme element
On dark backgrounds, accent text should be a single solid color (purple or blue), not gradient
Cards with rounded corners and pill labels are light theme patterns — on dark backgrounds, use flat layout instead
The Charcoal featured blocks within a light theme layout create emphasis through contrast — limit to 1-2 per page

Typography
Typeface: Avenir Next World
Fallback stack (digital): DM Sans (Google Fonts), system sans-serif
Monospace accent: Space Mono (Google Fonts) — for labels, badges, metadata, category tags, and technical content
Weights:

Regular: Long-form copy, body text
Demi: Headers, sub-headers, key callouts (primary weight)
Bold: Sparingly for maximum impact, campaign lines

Sizing Scale (digital):

Hero title: 48-56px, monospace or primary bold, tight letter-spacing (-1 to -2px)
Section header: 20-24px, primary bold
Body: 13-15px, primary regular
Labels/badges: 10-12px, monospace, uppercase, letter-spacing 1.5-3px
Meta/captions: 11-12px, primary regular, mid-gray color

Casing:

Primary: Sentence case
Headers/Sub-headers: ALL CAPS or Title Case permitted
Labels and badges: ALL CAPS with letter-spacing
Quotes/Statistics: Sentence case or ALL CAPS

Justification:

Left-aligned (standard for English)
Right-aligned (RTL languages)
Center alignment: sparingly, small text volumes only

Imagery Guidelines
Product on Devices

Use latest device models
Blank background with subtle shadow
Consistent lighting and reflections
Dynamic angles for hero moments
Flat-on perspective for tutorials

Product in Real Life

Show natural interactions (tapping, swiping, typing)
Locally relevant environments
Clean, professional workspaces
Visible, engaging device screens

People & Customers

Authentic, unscripted moments
People engaged in tasks, not posing
No direct eye contact with camera
Real workspaces (home offices, co-working, cafés)
Warm, natural lighting
Diverse, global representation

Image Treatment

Cut-outs must be framed (circle or rounded square)
Maximum one cut-out per layout
Not for use in templates by non-designers

Graphic Elements
The Circle

Connected to brand Halo
Use with clear purpose
Can be solid, outline, or dotted pattern
Use sparingly to add value
Dark theme: low-opacity outline circles as decorative background elements
Light theme: not used as floating decorative elements — circle language expressed through rounded card corners and pill shapes

The Line

Long/thin for subtle elegance
Short/bold for strong separation
Creates hierarchy and guides reading
Light theme: 1px Light Gray (#EEEEF2) for content dividers

The Box

Rounded corners (references Halo)
Highlights important information
Houses imagery
Creates content distinction
Light theme: 12-14px border radius, Off-White fill, 1px Light Gray border
Dark theme: less prominent — content typically sits on flat dark background

The Pill

Category labels and metadata badges
Tinted background (category color at ~15% opacity) with category-color text
4px rounded corners
Monospace font, uppercase, letter-spacing 1.5px
Light theme element — do not use on dark backgrounds

The Gradient Title

Multi-color gradient text: Electric Blue → Neon Purple → Halo Orange
Applied via CSS background-clip or equivalent
Light theme only — for hero titles and featured headings
Dark theme equivalent: solid Neon Purple or solid Electric Blue text

Tone of Voice Pillars
1. Keep it Simple

Use familiar, straightforward language
Avoid jargon and internal terms
No cultural/region-specific references
Break text into small sections

2. Be Concise

Lead with most important information
Avoid long preambles
Keep sentences short and single-minded
Remove unnecessary words

3. Create Connections

Use intelligent wit
Make messages memorable
Avoid clichés
Be smart but never self-indulgent
Straight-talking but never blunt
Accessible but never generic

4. Inspire Action

Use confidence-inspiring language
Provide proof through examples
Never exaggerate or overhype
Clear, specific CTAs (avoid vague "discover more")

Employer Brand Distinctions
Color Usage (Different from main brand)

Black & White: 60-80%
Hazy Pink (Secondary): 20-30% (dedicated employer brand color)
Tertiary colors: Maximum 10%

Gradients

Dark Gradient: Charcoal Black + Midnight Blue
Light Gradient: Pure White + Hazy Pink
Accent Gradient: Electric Blue + Neon Purple (light theme CTAs and banners)
Title Gradient: Electric Blue + Neon Purple + Halo Orange (light theme hero text)
Background use only, not for text/icons (except Title Gradient which is specifically for text)

Wave Pattern

Four unique dot-formation patterns
Represents global teams in unison
One pattern per asset
Full-bleed backgrounds or framed in circles/boxes
