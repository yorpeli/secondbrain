04 Focus 4 of 6 · Risk / Compliance

AHA — Audit Login Attempts of Risky Payees

Close the visibility gap created when bad actors pivot to the AHA path after the release-block control.
OKR Flawless at the Fundamentals OKR Keep Our Brand Trusted TFL ~$40.5M Closed AHs 15.2K
Background
We now block Payee-ID release from Risk-blocked AHs. Bad actors pivot to the AHA path — attaching the same Payee ID to a new AH.
The gap
The system blocks the attempt before login completes, so the new suspicious AH is never identified — we lose the audit trail.
The ask
Allow login to complete first (establishing the link to the new AH), then show "request cancelled" and block reuse — generating an audit trail required by the VP of Compliance for regulatory defensibility.
Scale
~$40.5M TFL across 15.2K closed accounts attributed to this typology.

05 Focus 5 of 6 · Payee Activation

Payees Journey Control

Gate payee activation on full onboarding, and shift payee actions & communications to the Enterprise team.
OKR Flawless at the Fundamentals OKR Keep Our Brand Trusted
The issue
Payees can complete AHA and receive money before the AH finishes KYC — a gap of hours to days between payee activation and registration completion. Partners read an active payee status as a green light to send funds.
Today
This generic activation fires for all programs except those with KYC-as-a-service (currently eBay / Best Buy). We need a default that gates activation on onboarding completion.
The ask
Gate payee activation on full onboarding completion, and shift payee actions and communications (IPCN events + email notifications) to the Enterprise team.
Reference
"AHA — activating balances before onboarding approval" (email + transcript)
Source: CLM Onboarding — Payees activation meeting.
