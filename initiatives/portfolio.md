## Thematic Overlaps

### 1. Foundry graduates → AIR² experiment runners
Foundry Builders produce PMs who can build AI tools and workflows. AIR² needs people who can move fast with AI experiments. The Foundry is training the workforce AIR² needs. Timing aligns — Foundry launches mid-April, AIR² ramping now.

### 2. AIR² as the live test bed for AI Team OS
AI Team OS is designing how AI-native teams should work but hasn't tested the model. AIR² — a fast-moving, AI-first team — could be the first live experiment. Instead of theorizing about team structure, run AIR² as the proof point and feed learnings back into the framework.

### 3. "Developing for Agents" connects all three
Oren flagged this for Foundry (both tracks). AI Team OS's framework assumes agents as first-class teammates. AIR² experiments will likely produce agent-driven solutions. This theme cuts across everything.

### 4. Foundry enables the AI Team OS transition path
AI Team OS's biggest open question is the transition from current state to AI-native teams. The Foundry is the enablement mechanism — it upskills the org. The transition path may be: Foundry Makers → Foundry Builders → ready for AI-native team placement.

### 5. AIR² experiments → Foundry case studies
Real AIR² experiments (vendor management agent, India team environment setup) become concrete case studies for Foundry Sessions 6, 10, 12 (PM use cases, capstones, team workflows). Better than hypothetical exercises.

### 6. External research feeds multiple initiatives
AI Team OS's external input (Wix: Builder/Creator split, repo readiness; Cellebrite: git-centric workflows, DonnyAgent) is directly relevant to how AIR² should set up its working environment and tools.

## Tensions to Watch

- **Speed vs. framework**: AIR² wants to move fast. AI Team OS wants to define the model first. Resolution: run AIR² as a live experiment of AI Team OS principles, but don't block AIR² on framework completion.
- **Three sponsors, one owner**: Different exec expectations require Yonatan to manage upward in three directions. Periodic alignment needed.
- **Tal's bandwidth**: Contributing meaningfully to three initiatives requires deliberate scoping of his role in each.
- **Infrastructure drift**: AIR² was explicitly repositioned away from infrastructure (Oren rejected that framing). If AI Team OS recommendations push toward infrastructure-first, that conflicts with AIR²'s mandate to ship, not build platforms.
