## Role 1: Product Manager — AIR²

### The Opportunity

We're building something different. AIR² is Payoneer's AI experimentation engine — a small, dedicated team with one mandate: **ship AI-powered initiatives, fast.**

No roadmap committees. No quarterly planning cycles. No waiting for platform teams. You'll take an idea from discovery to a working product in under 3 weeks — then decide with the team whether to scale it or kill it, and move on to the next one.

This isn't a "let's explore AI" initiative. It's a get-things-done initiative. You will have a dedicated engineering resource, and a clear operating framework. What it needs now is a PM who can match the pace.

### What You'll Do

- **Own the experiment pipeline end-to-end.** You'll source ideas (from across the org and your own observations), evaluate which ones are worth building, define the MVP scope, and drive the build-measure-decide cycle.
- **Run the validation loop.** Discovery → Build MVP → Run in production → Keep or Toss. You'll be running multiple experiments at different stages simultaneously.
- **Be the "value lens."** Engineers will push what's technically possible. You'll push what's actually valuable. Every experiment needs a clear hypothesis and a measurable outcome — you make sure that's never fuzzy.
- **Ship, not spec.** You'll write just enough to align the team and get building. If your longest document is more than a page, something went wrong.
- **Drive the Keep/Toss decision.** You'll gather the data, frame the trade-offs, and facilitate the group decision on whether an experiment scales, iterates, or dies.
- **Connect experiments to business impact.** You'll quantify the "Innovation Delta" — what does this save us? What does it unlock? You translate experiment results into a language leadership cares about.

### What Makes This Role Different

This isn't a typical PM role at Payoneer. There's no legacy system to maintain, no multi-quarter roadmap to negotiate, no dependency chains to manage. You'll operate more like a PM at a startup

## Role 2: Software Engineer — AIR²

### The Opportunity

AIR² is Payoneer's AI experimentation engine — a small, dedicated team with one mandate: **build AI-powered initiatives and ship them to production, fast.**

No long planning phases. No platform roadmaps. No waiting for other teams. You'll take an idea from discovery to a working product in under 3 weeks — build it, ship it, learn from it, move on.

This isn't a "let's explore AI" initiative. It's a get-things-done initiative. What we need is an engineer who'd rather ship something imperfect on Tuesday than plan something perfect for next quarter.

### What You'll Do

- **Build AI-powered MVPs end-to-end.** From prototype to production in days, not months. You'll work with LLMs, agents, APIs, and whatever tools get the job done fastest.
- **Own your initiatives.** This isn't "build what the PM spec'd." You'll be deeply involved in discovery — understanding the problem, shaping the solution, and making technical bets on what's feasible within the timeframe.
- **Ship to production — for real.** "Demo-ware" doesn't count here. What you build will run against real data, serve real users (internal or external), and produce measurable results.
- **Work within enterprise guardrails.** We move fast but we don't cut corners on security and compliance. Speed and safety aren't trade-offs here — they're both requirements.
- **Iterate or kill, quickly.** After each run, the team decides: scale it or toss it. No emotional attachment to code. The next initiative is already waiting.
- **Help stand up the experimentation environment.** Early on, you'll help define the sandbox, tooling, and development workflows that let the team move at AIR² speed.

### What Makes This Role Different

This isn't a typical engineering role at Payoneer. You won't be maintaining a legacy codebase or shipping incremental features on a quarterly cycle. Every few weeks you'll be working on something entirely new. The tech stack is whatever gets the job done — LLMs, agent frameworks, rapid prototyping tools, modern development environments. You'll operate more like an engineer at a startup.
